## The scenario

You've just finished a heat. You've got maybe fifteen minutes before your next session is called. You want to watch your fastest lap — not the whole heat, not the average lap, _the_ fastest one — and figure out where you left time.

RaceDash is built exactly for this.

## Step by step

1. **Import the session.** Drop your footage into the project and connect your timing source. RaceDash processes it in the background while you grab water.
2. **Jump to the fastest lap.** In the editor timeline, your fastest lap is highlighted in accent blue. Click the lap marker or press `B` to jump straight to the first frame.
3. **Scrub and learn.** Use the timeline scrubber or the arrow keys to step through the lap. The timing overlay on the right shows lap time, position and sector as you go.
4. **Export the lap only.** If you want to share it or keep it for review, open Render Assets and tick _"Fastest lap only"_. RaceDash will export just that lap with the overlay baked in. Thirty seconds on a local render, less if you're on cloud.

## Keyboard shortcuts

- `B` — jump to the fastest lap
- `N` — next lap
- `P` — previous lap
- `Space` — play/pause
- `←` / `→` — step one frame
- `Shift + ← / →` — step ten frames
