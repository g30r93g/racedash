RaceDash is a tool for turning race footage into broadcast-grade video, with timing and overlays baked in. It exists because a few of us got tired of spending our Sunday evenings in Premiere Pro trying to find the one lap we actually wanted to watch.

We race at Club100. We know the feeling of pulling into the paddock, grabbing your printed timing sheet, and scrolling through 45 minutes of camera footage on a phone, trying to work out which twenty seconds corresponds to your fastest lap. By the time you've found it, the next session is already on the grid.

RaceDash fixes that. You drop in your footage and your timing, RaceDash lines everything up automatically, and your fastest lap is highlighted with a single click. If you want to share it, the overlays are baked in and the render runs in the background while you get your kit on for the next run.

We're close to launching. The waitlist is open — if you'd like to be among the first to try it, drop your email on the [homepage](/) and we'll be in touch.

See you at the track.
